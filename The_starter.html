The flour

The choice of the flour is individually variable. You can use either all-purpose white and hard type, whole grain, or mixture of these.

Method

To begin wit hour starter we mix thoroughly 20g of flour with 20ml of water. Cover the jar so that is not vey tight however, the lid is able to stop the mixture to dry out. We let mixture to rest roughly 24 hours.

On the second day we repect the very same step and let the mixture to rest another day.

On the third day the only difference is the increased amount of flour and water. Already we can notice some boubles.

On the fourth day we repeat the third day process. The amount of boubles has increased considerably.

The chahge comes at day 5 when we start the feeding by taking out about one table spoon of mixture and throw it away. After that we add the amount of flour and water which can be seen in the table.

The sixth day is the last before our starter is powefulenough to be used to make a bread. We repeat the same steps as in day 5 and let the mixture to rest for another day. After the sixth day the starter is ready to be used, meaning that has enough and the right type of micbrobes to be able to fermentate a whole bread.
